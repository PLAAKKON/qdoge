Abstract

We present QDOGE (Quantum Doge), a novel decentralized token built on the Solana blockchain that leverages community-driven mechanisms for value propagation. Our approach combines the viral nature of meme-based assets with Solana's high-throughput, low-latency consensus mechanism. We demonstrate that QDOGE achieves transaction finality in <400ms with fees approaching $0.00025 per transaction. The token implements a fixed supply model with transparent on-chain governance.

Keywords: Solana, Meme Token, Decentralized Finance, Community Token, SPL Token

1. Introduction

The emergence of meme-based cryptocurrencies has demonstrated the power of community-driven value creation in decentralized systems. Unlike traditional assets, meme tokens derive their value from social consensus and viral propagation mechanisms [1].

In this paper, we introduce QDOGE, a Solana-based token that aims to establish a new paradigm in community-owned digital assets. The Solana blockchain provides the ideal substrate for QDOGE due to its:

  • High throughput: 65,000+ TPS theoretical maximum
  • Low latency: ~400ms block time
  • Minimal fees: ~$0.00025 per transaction
  • Proof of History consensus mechanism

1.1 Problem Statement

Let V(t) denote the value of a meme token at time t. Traditional models fail to capture the non-linear dynamics of community-driven assets. We propose a belief propagation model where:

V(t+1) = V(t) · ∏i∈N(t) mi→t(x)

where mi→t represents the message (belief) passed from holder i to the network at time t.

2.1 Supply Mechanics

QDOGE implements a deflationary mechanism through transaction burns:

S(t+1) = S(t) - β · T(t)

where S(t) is the total supply at time t, T(t) is transaction volume, and β is the burn rate coefficient.
